How to Navigate Around the Map (Back to the top of the page)

There are two main tools that allow you to navigate around the map:

Use the arrow buttons in the blue PAN TOOL to move North, South, East or West. Each time you click an arrow button the map will move in that direction. To move further, keep clicking the button.

If you are trying to zoom to a certain location, center the map on that location using the PAN TOOL and then use the ZOOM TOOL.
The ZOOM TOOL can either be used by clicking on the plus or minus buttons or by sliding the zoom bar up or down. WARNING: the ZOOM TOOL is sensitive and it is easy to zoom in or out more than you wish by sliding the bar.

There are other options to navigate the map. If you have a scroll wheel on your mouse, this will zoom the map in and out.

Additionally, AS LONG AS YOU ARE NOT DRAWING YOUR ROUTE OR ACTIVITY AREA AT THE TIME, you can place your mouse over the map and then click and hold your left mouse button. This allows you to drag the map.

How to Change Your Base Map (Back to the top of the page)

We have provided a number of different maps (or base layers) to help you identify your route and your activity areas. You can swap between these as required.

To change your base map simply click on a different base layer in the blue box in the top right of your screen.

There are three available maps: nautical charts, satellite images and general terrain.

WARNING: for technical reasons there are limits to how far you can zoom in using either the charts or terrain map. If these will not display as you zoom in, please turn on the satellite map.

Additionally, both the satellite and terrain maps can be zoomed out to show the whole of the US and beyond. The available nautical charts are limited to the North-east US.

 

 

 

 

How to Draw Your Route (Back to the top of the page)

The instructions on how to draw you route may seem complicated but once you have tried it, you should find it quite easy.

NOTE: Feel free to play around and try it out and don't worry if you make a mistake. You can restart your route at any time by clicking the REDRAW BUTTON. YOU WILL ALSO HAVE THE OPPORTUNITY TO EDIT OR REDRAW YOUR ROUTE BEFORE YOU SAVE IT.
Click once on the map where you started your trip.
 
Move mouse and click to create a second point (or waypoint).
 
Continue clicking to create your route.
 

 

Use the PAN TOOL and the ZOOM TOOL to move the map as you go.

NOTE: as you go to use the tools, your route will appear to "follow" you. When you click on either of the tools, it WILL NOT CREATE A WAYPOINT. Even if you do create a point by mistake, you will be able to delete it later by choosing to edit your route.
 

Once you have finished drawing your ROUND TRIP, double-click the last point to complete the route.

NOTE: if you double-click by accident before you have completed drawing your route, you will be offered the opportunity to CONTINUE DRAWING YOUR ROUTE.
 

If you not happy with your route, click the REDRAW BUTTON.

NOTE: you will also have the opportunity to edit or redraw your route before you save it.

How to Edit your Route Once you have Drawn It (Back to the top of the page)

Once you have finished drawing your route, you will have a final opportunity to edit it by adding, moving, or removing waypoints before you save the final route.

NOTE: once you have saved the route it is not possible to edit it.

Again, the instructions may seem complicated but once you have tried it, you should find it quite easy.

Once you have selected to edit your route:
The dark circles you see on your route are the waypoints you created when drawing the route.
 
The light circles between your waypoints are 'ghost' points and are used to create new waypoints.
 
MOVING A WAYPOINT - To move to a waypoint, click on it and hold your mouse button down while dragging it where you want. Then release your mouse button. Repeat as necessary until you are satisfied with where the point is.
 
ADDING A WAYPOINT - To add a waypoint click a 'ghost' point and drag it where you want, then release your mouse button.
 
REMOVING A WAYPOINT - To remove a waypoint place your mouse over it (the cursor will change to a 4-way arrow) and press the DELETE key on your keyboard.
Click the DONE EDITING BUTTON when you are happy with the changes that you have made. This will save your route.
How to Place an Activity Point (Back to the top of the page)

Placing an activity point is very similar to how you drew your route except you only click once.

NOTE: Feel free to play around and try it out and don't worry if you make a mistake. YOU WILL HAVE THE OPPORTUNITY TO RE-PLOT YOUR POINT BEFORE YOU SAVE IT.
Click once on the map to place your activity point.
